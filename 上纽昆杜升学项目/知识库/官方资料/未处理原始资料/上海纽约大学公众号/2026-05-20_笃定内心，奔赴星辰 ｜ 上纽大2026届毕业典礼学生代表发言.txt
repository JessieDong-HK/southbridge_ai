2026届本科毕业生
Fatima Zahra Foutouh
感恩陪伴与机遇，
笃定内心，勇敢向前
“

尊敬的校领导、来宾、家长，以及今天的主角——2026届毕业生们：
“你好”，是我刚到上海时，唯一会说的中文。四年过去，我的中文比当初进步了不少，也有故事想和大家分享。
今天，能站在这里，作为2026届毕业生代表发言，我深感荣幸。而回望这一切，我们每个人的故事，似乎都始于同一个瞬间——走进未知。
刚来到这里时，我们大都既兴奋、又不安。有人第一次到上海，有人第一次接触中文，有人第一次面对新的环境、新的期待，以及新的人生篇章。但过去四年里，上海在不知不觉中成了我们的家。
家是什么？是玉兰庭里的欢声笑语；是凌晨两点的图书馆；是DDL截止前五分钟模型终于跑通的如释重负；是那些让你怀疑自己的代码、甚至开始怀疑人生的Bug（故障）。
在这里，同学变成伙伴，伙伴变成挚友。我们也一起，把上海纽约大学塑造成了现在的模样——这里有不同背景的人、有好奇心、有真实的联结。
但说到底，上海纽约大学带给我们的，从来不只是课堂。起初，我们或许是冲着学位来的，但真正让我们舍不得离开的，是那些无法被量化的东西。
感谢那些一直关心我们的老师。他们在意的，是教学，更是我们每个人。我忘不了迷茫时他们给予的耐心与鼓励。他们教给我们的，从来不只是课本上的知识，还有如何思考、保持好奇，以及敢于质疑的勇气。
感谢校园里的阿姨、师傅们。他们可能不知道，在那些最疲惫甚至狼狈的清晨，他们温暖的笑容，曾给了我多大的力量。
还要感谢那些发生在你我生命里的机会。去纽约、阿布扎比、伦敦、巴黎海外交流（Study Away）；泡在实验室里探究自己内心的好奇；和来自七十多个国家和地区的同学们共同学习……在上纽大，我们不只在课堂上学习跨文化交际，我们身临其境、我们耳濡目染，也在彼此的差异里，重新认识这个世界、重新认识自己。
我们学着像科学家那样思考和解决问题；学会与不确定性共处，不断试错、调整，再重新开始。后来我才发现，训练模型是这样，成长也是。
上海纽约大学始终希望培养的，是带着好奇心、责任感与目标感走向世界的人，是愿意不断探索、不断发现意义的人。而今天的我们，不只是这一愿景下的毕业生。我们本身就是它最好的证明。
我们拥有最优秀的老师、最温暖的社群，以及如此珍贵的机会，但我们依然会在人生某些时刻发现，最难战胜的，从来都是内心的挣扎。所以今天，我想把一句话送给大家：“比起失败，自我怀疑会扼杀更多的梦想。”请大家想一想，是不是这么回事——让梦想破灭的，不是失败，是怀疑。
失败不可怕，大不了我们重头再来。但怀疑不同，怀疑会让你连试都不敢试，让你错过那个更好的自己——不是因为你做不到，而是因为你太早就断定你不行。
还有一件事，毕业典礼上很少有人真地提起：我们每一个人，都曾听见过那个声音。它说“你不属于这里”，它说“你还不够好”。我听过，你们也一定听过，我们都听过。可现在，请大家看看彼此。我们，好好地站在这里。
最难的时候，我们没有逃跑；最累的时候，我们没有放弃；一次次被击垮，又一次次把信念拼回来。而这一切，都是我们在异国他乡、没有家人陪伴、扛着巨大压力下做到的。这样的我们，值得为自己骄傲。
所以未来，当日子再次变得难熬——毕竟人生不顺十之八九——请记得今天，记得此时此刻、此情此景里的每个人。如我刚刚所说，我们已经战胜过人生中最难应付的对手：那个藏在内心深处的自我怀疑。
你曾经做到了，你就还会再做到。
最后，我想把这一时刻也送给父母，尤其是我的父亲。我的榜样不是某位遥不可及的所谓“大人物”，我的榜样一直是他——那个当我都怀疑自己时，还愿意相信我的人。所以今天这张学位证书上，写的不只是我的名字，还有你们一直以来的爱与信任。爸爸妈妈，这份荣誉同样属于你们。
同时，我也想感谢今天来到现场的每一位亲朋。是你们一路陪伴我们走到了这里，这一刻，也同样属于你们。2026届的同学们，请给自己一点时间——转过身，看看那些一直支持着你的人，让他们和你一起分享此刻的喜悦。
走到今天，我们不是为了就此止步。这里不是终点，是起点。
2026届，毕业快乐！向着未来，出发！

University Leadership, distinguished guest, family, and most importantly, the Class of 2026,
你好 (Nihao) was the first and only Chinese word I knew when I landed in Shanghai.
Now, four years later, I have a few more words, and a story to tell.
It’s an honor to stand here today, representing the Class of 2026. And while my journey started with just one word, all of ours started with something in common: stepping into the unknown.
Back then, most of us arrived with excitement... and a little bit of fear. For many of us, it was a new city, a new language. For others, it was a new environment, new expectations, a new chapter.
But over the past four years, Shanghai slowly became something none of us expected: home. It's the laughter at Magnolia Cafe?, the 2 a.m. library sessions, the nights when your model finally converges... until five minutes before the deadline. It's the problem sets that made you question not just your code, but your entire career choice. Classmates became teammates, teammates became friends, and we built something that truly defines NYU Shanghai: a community shaped by diversity, curiosity, and connection.
But honestly? This place was never just about the classes. I came here for the degree. I stayed for everything else.
The faculty who actually cared, not just about the syllabus, but about us. The ones who nurtured something in us: critical thinking, intellectual curiosity, the courage to question.
And the ayis and the shifus, whose warm smile on my worst mornings carried me more than they'll ever know.
And then the opportunities: New York, Abu Dhabi, London, Paris, research labs, students from over 70 countries. I didn't just learn cross-cultural communication here. I lived it.
We learned to think like scientists and problem solvers, to sit with uncertainty, iterate, fail, and try again. Turns out that's not just how you train a model. That's how you build a life.
NYU Shanghai's vision was always to prepare us for lives of discovery and meaning, leaders who move through the world with curiosity, integrity, and purpose. We are not just graduates of that vision. We are proof that it works.
And yet, even with the best faculty, the warmest community, and the greatest opportunities, there were still moments when the hardest battle was the one inside our own heads.
There is a quote I want to leave with you: "Doubt kills more dreams than failure ever will." Let that sink in. Not failure. Doubt.
Failure we can survive, it teaches us, it gives us another draft. But doubt convinces you to never even start. Doubt is the person you never became, not because you couldn't, but because you told yourself you couldn't.
And here is the truth nobody talks about at graduations: every single one of us has heard that voice.
The voice that said you don't belong here. That you're not enough. I heard it. You heard it. We all did.
But look around you. We are still here.
We stayed when it was hard. We showed up when we were exhausted. We rebuilt when things fell apart. And we did it, in a foreign city, far from home, under pressure that would have broken a lesser version of us. You did it once. You will do it again.
So when life gets hard, and it will, remember this room. Remember that you have already defeated the hardest opponent you will ever face: the doubt inside your own mind.
Before I close, I want to dedicate this moment to my parents, especially my dad. I don't have a world leader who inspires me. I have him. A man who never stopped believing in me, even when I stopped believing in myself. This degree carries your belief as much as it does my name. Mom, Dad, this one's for you.
And to every family and friend in this room, thank you. You carried us here. This moment is yours too. So graduates, take a moment—turn to the people who supported you… and let them feel this moment with you.
We didn't come this far to only come this far. This is not our finish line. This is our starting point. Class of 2026, let's go!
2026届硕士毕业生 周毛头
在更广阔的天空，如星辰闪耀

尊敬的校领导、各位来宾、各位2026届毕业生，大家好。
今天，能够作为硕博毕业生代表站在这里发言，我深感荣幸。这样特别的时刻，离不开许多人的努力。因此，我想首先把最真挚的感谢送给他们。
感谢学校的工作人员。你们无数次的默默付出，让校园每一天都有条不紊，也让我们能踏实学习、安心生活。
感谢每一位老师。谢谢你们的悉心指导，也谢谢你们不断鼓励我们向前，成为更好的自己。
感谢一路同行的伙伴们。谢谢你们带来的欢笑，也谢谢你们一直坚定地陪在我们身边，走过低谷与荣耀。
也感谢我们的家人、我们爱的人。是你们一次次的包容、付出与支持，托举着我们一路走到了今天。
也感谢每一位以自己的方式——无论大小——塑造了这座校园的人。无论是被看见的付出，还是那些细微、温暖的小举动，都让上海纽约大学成为了一个真正包容我们、接纳我们的地方。
今天站在这里，回想这一切，心中只有感慨。
这是怎样的一年呢？这一年里，我们有过迷茫，也经历了许多措手不及的时刻。而在这一切之中，我们也更加深刻地体会到，人与人之间的支持与陪伴是多么可贵。但无论发生了什么，今天的我们，一起走到了这里。
这座校园，从来不只是一个学习的地方。它更像一个家，让原本陌生的我们慢慢靠近。我们在这里讲故事、找灵感；我们在这里造梦。我们之中，有人从本科开始就在这念书，也有人到硕博阶段与上纽大结缘。无论我们是初次扬帆，还是再度启程，今天的我们，都早已被写进同一个故事里。
所以，这就引出我今天想和大家聊的主题——星星。在浩瀚夜空中，有一颗名为牛郎星（Altair）的星，正静静闪耀。夜幕降临时，它是最明亮的星辰之一。几个世纪以来，无数人仰望过它。航海者靠它在海上辨认方向，旅人循着它穿越陌生的地方。星星不会帮你划船，也不会替你赶路，但它会提醒你，该往哪走。
某种意义上，我们身边的人，也像夜空中明亮的星辰，默默指引着我们。他们没有替我们走完人生的路，却让我们在迷茫的时候，看见了自己能够前行的方向。
从今天开始，我们要走向一片未知的星空了。世界一下子变得更加辽阔，而我们也将像银河中的群星一样，奔赴各自的方向。
有人会去往新的城市，开始一段陌生又期待已久的生活；有人会进入新的行业，迎接新的挑战；有人会组建自己的家庭，步入人生新的阶段。也有人，还在路上，还在寻找。但这都没关系。因为重要的是，我们仍在前行、在成长，在迷茫时愿意仰望星空、抬眼远方。
未来一定会有计划改变的时候，也一定会有不如意的时候。但那不代表失败，它们同样是人生旅程的一部分，也正是这些经历，让我们成为今天的自己。最有意义的际遇，往往来自计划之外。
当我们真正迈向下一段旅程时，我希望我们还能记得这几件事情。
第一，别断了联系。也许很多年后，你会发现，今天坐在你身边的人，仍然是这个世界上最懂你的人。一通电话、一条消息，又或者是短暂小聚，都足以让我们的心紧紧相连。
第二，去做你觉得有意义的事情，也别忘了时常问问自己，当初为什么出发。去拥抱那些能让你突破自己的挑战。去成长，也去尽可能地照亮别人，哪怕只是一点点微弱的光。
最后，也请大家别忘记：在这段旅程里，你从来都不是孤身一人。很多年后，我们或许早已忘记课堂上的某些内容，也记不清那些熬夜完成的作业。但我们会记得彼此交谈时的那份真诚。记得那些一起大笑、一起迷茫、一起成长的时刻。也会记得，曾经有那么一段时间，我们共同属于这里。
所以，当我们走向下一程时，希望今天的这些瞬间与感受，能一直陪伴你们。当未来模糊不清时，请记得，总会有一束光指引你继续前行。而当别人迷失的时候，我们也能成为那束光。
感谢过去我们一起创造的回忆、联系与故事。未来的天空很大，愿我们都能在各自的位置上，静静闪耀。
谢谢大家，也祝2026届的我们，毕业快乐。

University Leadership and Distinguished Guests, hello and congratulations Class of 2026. I feel tremendously honored to be here today, representing the Masters and PhD candidates. I want to take a moment to recognize the people who made this day possible.
To our administrators and staff, thank you for the countless unseen efforts that kept this community running. To our professors and teachers, thank you for your patience and challenging us to grow.
To our friends, thank you for the joy and silly times, and also being there for the lows and highs. To our families and loved ones, thank you for your countless sacrifices and your constant support that has carried us to this moment.
And to everyone who has shaped this community in ways both big and small, thank you for helping build a place that welcomed who we are.
So I begin by saying, “Oh wow. Oh wow. Oh wow.”
What a year it has been. A year that has tested us, surprised us, and reminded us just how much we rely on one another. And despite everything else, we made it here today… together.
Because this campus has been more than just a place to study. It has been a second home where stories were shared, ideas were made, and dreams began to take shape.
Some of us began here at the start of our academic journeys, and others joined later on, but what matters is that, whether we’re stepping out for the first time or once again, we are all part of the same story.
But today, I also want to talk about stars.
There is a star right above us called Altair, one of the brightest stars in the night sky.
For centuries, people have looked to this star for guidance.
Sailors used it to navigate oceans. Travelers used it to find their way across lands.
Stars don’t move the ship or your feet for you, but they can give you direction.
In many ways, the people around us have been our guiding stars.
They didn’t walk the path for us, but they helped us see where we could go.
Because after today, it feels like entering an unknown sky.
The world suddenly becomes larger and splits us like the Milky Way.
Some of us will move to new cities. Some will start new careers. Some will start families. And others may still be figuring it out.
And that’s okay.
Because what matters is that we keep moving, keep learning, and keep looking up especially when things feel uncertain.
There will be times when plans change, or when things don’t work out.
But those moments are not failures. They are part of the journey that shapes us.
Sometimes, the most meaningful paths are the ones we never planned.
As we set foot into what’s ahead, there’s a few things I hope we all remember.
First, stay connected. Years from now, the people sitting around you today may still be the ones who understand you best.
Call them. Message them. Visit them. Stay in touch.
Second, do work that means something to you, and ask why you do it.
Find something that challenges you. Something that pushes you to grow. Something that helps others, even if it’s a small kind gesture.
And finally, remember this: You do not walk this journey alone.
We may not remember every class we took, or every assignment we completed.
But we will remember the conversations. The feelings. The joy and laughter. The sense that, for a brief moment, we were all here together.
So as we begin the next journey, I hope you remember these lessons.
And when times feel uncertain, look up to the light that guides you.
When someone else is lost, be the light that guides them.
Thank you all for the memories, the connection, and the story we shared.
The sky ahead of us is vast
But let us all become stars.
Thank you all very much and congratulations, Class of 2026.
